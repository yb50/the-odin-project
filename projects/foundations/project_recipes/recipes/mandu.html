Mandu

Description

Mandu are Korean dumplings typically filled with a flavorful mixture of meat and vegetables. They can be steamed, boiled, pan-fried, or deep-fried, and are often served with a soy-vinegar dipping sauce. Mandu are enjoyed year-round, but are especially popular during holidays like Lunar New Year.

Fillings can vary widely, from ground pork or beef to tofu and kimchi, making them versatile and suitable for different diets. Mandu can be served as a main dish, side dish, or in soups such as mandu-guk, a comforting dumpling soup.

Ingredients

  • Dumpling wrappers
  • Ground pork or beef
  • Tofu (optional)
  • Kimchi (chopped and drained)
  • Garlic
  • Green onions
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground meat, finely chopped kimchi, crumbled tofu, minced garlic, and chopped green onions.
  2. Season the filling with soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper, then mix thoroughly.
  3. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each dumpling wrapper.
  4. Moisten the edges with water, fold the wrapper in half, and press to seal tightly.
  5. To pan-fry: heat oil in a nonstick pan, add mandu, and cook until the bottoms are golden.
  6. Add a few tablespoons of water to the pan, cover, and steam for a few minutes until cooked through.

  8. Serve hot with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and a dash of sesame oil.

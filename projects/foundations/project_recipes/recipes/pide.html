Pide

Description

Pide is a popular Turkish flatbread dish, often described as Turkish pizza. It features a boat-shaped dough filled with a variety of toppings such as seasoned minced meat, cheese, or vegetables, and is baked until golden and crispy.

Whether served with spicy sausage (sucuk), ground lamb, or just a mix of cheeses and herbs, pide is a beloved street food and comfort meal across Turkey. It's commonly enjoyed hot out of the oven, sliced into pieces and shared among friends and family.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Warm water
  • Dry yeast
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Ground beef or lamb
  • Onion
  • Tomato paste
  • Green peppers
  • Grated cheese (optional)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix warm water, yeast, and a bit of sugar; let it sit until foamy.
  2. Combine with flour, salt, and olive oil to form a smooth dough. Let it rise for 1 hour.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare the filling by sautéing onions, green peppers, and ground meat in olive oil.
  4. Add tomato paste and cook until the mixture is thickened; season with salt and spices.
  5. Preheat your oven to 220°C.
  6. Divide the dough into portions, roll each into an oval or boat shape.
  7. Place the filling in the center of the dough, leaving the edges clear.
  8. Fold and pinch the edges to form a boat shape, then brush the crust with olive oil or egg yolk.
  9. Sprinkle with cheese if desired, then bake for 15–20 minutes until golden and crisp.
  10. Serve hot, sliced, with lemon wedges or fresh herbs on the side.

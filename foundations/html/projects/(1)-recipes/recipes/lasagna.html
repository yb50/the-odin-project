Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. It is typically baked until the top is golden and bubbling, creating a satisfying blend of textures and flavors.

While classic lasagna features ground beef and tomato sauce, many variations exist, including vegetarian and seafood versions. This hearty dish is often enjoyed as a main course and is a popular choice for family meals and gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Tomato sauce
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh basil

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 190°C.
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions and set them aside.
  3. In a skillet, sauté chopped onion and garlic in olive oil until softened.
  4. Add ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned.
  5. Stir in the tomato sauce and simmer for about 15 minutes.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix ricotta cheese with a bit of salt and chopped basil.
  7. Spread a layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Add a layer of noodles, then ricotta mixture, then mozzarella, and repeat the layers.
  9. Top the final layer with sauce and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese.
  10. Bake for 30–40 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  11. Let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
